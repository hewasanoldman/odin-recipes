General Tso's Chicken

General Tso's chicken is crispy, twice-fried chicken in a deliciously sweet and sticky glaze. This mouthwatering dish delivers a combination of Asian flavors that will knock your chopsticks off! Serve with steamed broccoli and white rice and garnish with sliced green onions.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 pinch white pepper
  • 1 cup cornstarch

Sauce

  • 4 cups vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 pinch white pepper
  • 1 cup cornstarch

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Whisk egg in large mixing bowl. Add chicken, salt, sugar, and pepper; mix well. Gradually mix in cornstarch until the chicken is well coated.
  3. Working in batches, carefully drop one piece of chicken at a time into the hot oil. Fry until chicken turns golden brown and begins to float, about 3 minutes. Remove chicken to a plate and allow to cool as you fry the next batch.
  4. Once all chicken has been fried, refry the chicken, starting with the first batch. Fry until chicken turns deep golden brown, about 2 more minutes. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  5. Prepare sauce: Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Stir in green onion, garlic, whole chilies, and orange zest. Cook and stir until garlic turns golden and chilies brighten, 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. Add sugar, soy sauce, chicken broth, peanut oil, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and ginger; bring to a boil and cook for 3 minutes. Dissolve 2 teaspoons cornstarch in water and stir into the sauce. Return to a boil and cook until the sauce thickens and is no longer cloudy, about 1 minute.
  7. Stir chicken into the boiling sauce. Reduce heat to low and cook until chicken is heated through and has absorbed some sauce, about 3 minutes.
